I'm gonna piss off a lot of older people by saying that I'm part of the first generation which doesn't really know life without the iPhone. I was born in 2002, so the iPhone released when I was 5 years old. However, I do remember the selling point of the phone was that it would become a "productivity device" to help all of us in our goals.

That selling point has seemed to be more of a mirage now. The most popular Apps are either shopping, social media, or AI apps. In my home country of the United States, it's gotten to the point where your phone of choice is tied to your social status than whether or not you use the phone. The amount of flak I've gotten from people for having a Android and not an iPhone is kinda hilarious considering I just bought whatever the cheapest phone was I could get. Phone addiction is through the roof, people's attention spans are show, and Gen Z is predicted to be hte first generation who's less smart than the previous one. In short, we're f*cked. completely
